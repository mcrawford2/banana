# What does it do
- gives supply chain information to consumers to teach about the complexities of every day items

# How do I use it
- scroll through the web app to learn about the banana's journey
- click on images with labels to see a closer up image of where the banana came from
- click on parts 1-6 of the supply chain to learn about each step of the process and see where in the world it heppen on the map
- type in the "Thank you to..." box to share a positive message of appreciation
- click the + or - buttons under 'Quick Reflection" section to make a guess on how many people helped get the banana to the grocery store

# What did you learn
- this was a very beneficial part of the project, because it allows consumers/users to become more engaged with what they are purchasing.
- making this allowed me to practice what we have learned about HTML, CSS, and Javascript in an independent way.
- it also gave me the chance to do something for a 'client' outside of the class, because this was presented to executives from a grocery store chain.
